Hydrological Parameters Explained

Our model evaluates several critical geological and atmospheric sensors to forecast local flood hazard probabilities:

  • Flood Probability: The probability calculated by the XGBoost ensemble classifier that water accumulation will exceed storm drainage capabilities.
  • Expected Duration: Synthesized by the LSTM recurrent neural network representing the time window of active cloud rainfall.
  • Cumulative Rainfall: The cumulative millimetric volume of rain expected over the sliding 72-hour period.
  • Peak Intensity: The highest expected hourly rainfall rate (in mm/hour), key in determining urban flash drainage bypass triggers.
  • Elevation (Local Zone): Metric distance above sea level. Lower coastal elevations are highly susceptible to backwater lockage.
  • Drainage Capacity: Architectural efficiency index representing stormwater pipeline discharge rates.

Historical Flood Event Validations

Our machine learning models are calibrated and rigorously tested against historical Chennai precipitation crises:

  • November/December 2015 Deluge: Extreme northeast monsoon rainfall matching ~350mm cumulative accumulation in 24 hours. Under this validation test, the model correctly triggers an immediate High Risk alert for lowland marsh fringes like Velachery and Pallikaranai.
  • December 2021 Cloudbursts: Unpredicted localized thunderstorms causing rapid 150mm accumulation in hours. The model successfully detects immediate soil moisture saturation risks in central dense flatlands like T. Nagar and Kodambakkam.
  • December 2023 Cyclone Michaung: Heavy coastal rain bands accompanied by tidal surges of ~1.2m. The model models low elevation coupled with elevated river discharge paths, predicting suburban waterlogging.
